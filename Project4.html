CAD - Supersonic Test Section

While attending Purdue University, in pursuit of my Masters of Mechanical Engineering, I worked with my colleagues Abigayle Moser, Chandler Moy, Dr. Terry Zhao, and Professor Sally Bane in the Purdue Aeroscience Laboratory to design a removable test section for experiments in the supersonic wind tunnel. This test section design aimed to simplify the manufacturing process somewhat and reduce the amount of material used compared to previous test block designs.

One of the major challenges in designing this unorthodox block were ensuring a strong attachment between the base of the assembly and the sample section on top while keeping a tight seal to prevent pressure leakage that would affect data collection. This was solved by placing o-rings around the copper pressure pipes that would expand in compression when the aluminum blocks were tightened together.

This design conserves material by reducing the amount of material needed to create a new test block for experiments, allowing more variation in design parameters for researchers to investigate. By designing one base and multiple interchangeable sample blocks, researchers can make almost 3.5 sample blocks with the same material that would have previously made one test section.
