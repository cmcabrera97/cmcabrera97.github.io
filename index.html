Cristiano Cabrera

About Me

I am Cristiano Cabrera, and I am a staff consultant at Engineering Systems Inc. in the Fire & Explosions Department based out of the Miami office. With a background focused primarily on experimental work with wind tunnel experiments and micro-scale manufacturing, I am expanding into the simulation and modeling of thermal systems and fluid flow phenomena for fire and explosion investigations.

Before joining ESi, I was a graduate research assistant and teaching assistant at Purdue University. My thesis focused on the application of bio-inspired surface coatings in supersonic flow control applications as well as comparison of micro-mesoscale manufacturing methods for fabricating bio-inspired surface coatings. As a teaching assistant, I worked with students on sophomore design projects involving research into existing designs and industry standards to develop safe and effective prototype products. I also assisted in the testing of projects and wrote lab manuals for a new Purdue University course curriculum that teaches students more hands-on fabrication skills.

Research Interests

Beyond my current work and research, I am deeply committed to exploring sustainable energy solutions as I believe renewable energy is key to addressing our planet's environmental and social challenges in creating a more sustainable future. I am also enthusiastic about education and the role it plays in empowering individuals and communities to tackle the problems they face in their daily lives.

I am driven by the belief that interdisciplinary collaboration is our most powerful tool for tackling the complex issues facing the world. The sharing of knowledge and information between researchers, policymakers, and industry experts is key to solving the hardest problems of our modern age. If you are interested in discussing potential research opportunities or collaborations, please do not hesitate to reach out to discuss how I might be able to contribute.
