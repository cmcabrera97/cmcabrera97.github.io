CAD - Latch Block

While attending Southwestern Illinois College, in pursuit of my Associates of Computer Aided Design, I was tasked with recreating a trailer coupler brought in by the professor. An image of a similar product is shown to the right.

I disassembled the assembled product, took measurements using tools such as rulers, calipers, micrometers, and round/fillet gages. I then drew each piece by hand on grid paper, labeling feature dimensions and locations to ensure thorough documentation of the design. I then recreated the design in SolidWorks first as a 3D model, and then put together the 2D drawings after.

Some of the challenges in taking measurements were the complexity of pieces such as the Latch Cover and Latch with the difficult to measure curved surfaces. During the 3D portion of this project, following along with my pre-planned drawings helped in ensuring that 2D sketches were fully defined from the start and gave me a better understanding for how each part fit together. In 2D, this was one of the easiest projects I have done since I already planned out how the drawings would be organized and how features were to be called out.
