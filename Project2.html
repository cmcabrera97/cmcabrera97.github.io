CAD - Any Angle Tool Vise

While attending Southwestern Illinois College, in pursuit of my Associates of Computer Aided Design, I completed the Any Angle Tool Vise exercise from 'Technical Drawing with Engineering Graphics' 14th Edition by Giesecke et al. This assignment was a collaborative project completed by myself and a classmate. We completed the assignment in SolidWorks first as 3D models, and then created the 2D drawings after.

Some of the challenges in the 3D portion of this project were the complexity of creating angled and offset features using reference planes, particularly in the Upper Plate and Lower Plate pieces, as well as working out how each piece fit together from a series of individual piece images and one perspective of the assembly. In 2D, there was a healthy mix of complex and basic pieces when evaluating how to dimension and call out features. This project was also an opportunity to work with different materials in the 3D viewport to help more accurately represent what the completed design would look like when manufactured.

This is the assembly of the Any Angle Tool Vise. It utilizes not only the ten unique pieces of the project, but also the five purchased parts found from an external vendor.

The Saddle is the main piece of the design, anchoring the more complex pieces of the vise to the work surface.

The Compound Center Member is one of the degrees of freedom in the design which allows the rotation of the piece in place on one planar axis.

The Upper Compound Member is the other degree of freedom in the design which allows the rotation of the piece in place on the second planar axis.

The Compound Tool Holder is the third degree of freedom in the design which allows the rotation of the piece in place on the vertical rotational axis.

The Upper Plate is a guide piece that limits the rotation in each of the planar axes. Three of them are used to include the right amount of clearnace for attaching fasteners.

The Lower Plate is a guide piece that limits the rotation in each of the planar axes. It is similar to the Upper Plate, but has more material that does not have the same clearnace along the top.

The Locking Handle is a piece used to loosen and tighten rotation of the vise. The design uses four handles, two on each axis, in order to securely lock the position of the vise.

The Eccentric is a piece that is mounted inside the main body of the design. The two diameters of the cylinder are slightly off-axis of each other to lock the vise in place.

The Protractor Scale is used to measure the angular offset of the vise in the vertical axis.

The Clamp Plug is used to help apply pressure from the screws into the Compound Tool Holder to control the vertical axis position.
